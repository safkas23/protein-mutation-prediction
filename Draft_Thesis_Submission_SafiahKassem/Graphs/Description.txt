Stability Distribution: 
Shows the distribution of protein stability scores in the dataset. 
This graph helps identify the overall range, central tendency, and any skewness in stability values, 
providing insight into how stable or unstable most proteins are.

Seq Length vs. Stability:
Illustrates the relationship between protein sequence length and stability score. 
This visualization helps determine whether longer or shorter protein sequences tend to be more stable and reveals any underlying trends or patterns.

Error Distribution:
Displays the distribution of prediction errors (difference between predicted and actual stability). 
This graph helps evaluate model performance by showing whether errors are centered around zero and whether large deviations occur frequently.

Predicted vs. Actual:
Compares predicted stability values to actual stability values. 
A strong alignment along the diagonal line indicates good model performance, 
while deviations from the line highlight prediction inaccuracies.
